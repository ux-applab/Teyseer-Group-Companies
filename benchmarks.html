Industry Verticals — Version 1 Open Page

4 section benchmarks across hero stats, industry panel, achievements grid, and end CTA.

Hero — Stat Strip
Aramco — "At a glance" minimal stats strip

The 4-column statistics strip anchored at the base of the v1 hero section was benchmarked from Aramco's "At a glance" section. The pattern uses large, light-weight numbers with a short label beneath and a subtle separator line — letting the numbers command attention without heavy visual decoration. Teyseer adapts this into a 4-column strip using brand blue for the stat values.

aramco.com Visit Source
Industries Served Section
Honeywell — "What We Do" left-nav with full-bleed content panel

The left-rail tab navigation pattern used in the Industry Verticals panel of v1 was benchmarked from Honeywell's "What We Do" section. A vertical list of industries on the left activates a full-bleed image content panel on the right with a floating description card — enabling users to explore many sectors without leaving the page. The active state uses a red left-border accent; Teyseer adapts this to brand blue.

honeywell.com Visit Source
Key Achievements Section
Al Futtaim — "Sustainability Highlights" 4-column image card grid

The "Key Achievements Across the Verticals" section in v1 was benchmarked from Al Futtaim's Sustainability Highlights on their Impact page. The pattern uses a 4-column image-led card grid where each card carries a photo, bold headline, and short description — presenting achievements as editorial content rather than a list. Teyseer adapts this into 3-column cards with a vertical tag, year, title, and description.

alfuttaim.com/impact Visit Source
End of Page CTA
Aramco — Full-bleed image CTA with centred headline

The end-of-page CTA section in v1 was benchmarked from Aramco's full-bleed "About us" banner. The pattern overlays a large, centred headline and a minimal arrow CTA on an atmospheric industrial photograph — creating a strong visual close to the page that communicates scale and credibility. Teyseer adapts this into a blue-gradient CTA with a headline, subtext, and two action buttons (Partner with Us / News).

aramco.com Visit Source

Industry Verticals — Version 2 Open Page

4 section benchmarks across hero stats, achievements grid, industry accordion, and end CTA.

Hero — Stat Strip
Aramco — "At a glance" minimal stats strip

The same Aramco "At a glance" pattern informs the v2 hero stats strip. In v2 the hero is more compact — prioritising getting the industry verticals accordion above the fold on larger screens — so the stats strip is tighter, with smaller padding. The large-number typographic style and bottom separator are retained from the Aramco benchmark.

aramco.com Visit Source
Key Achievements Section
Al Futtaim — "Sustainability Highlights" image card grid

The "Key Achievements Across the Verticals" section in v2 also draws from Al Futtaim's Sustainability Highlights card grid. In v2, the cards are arranged in a 3-column grid with a vertical tag indicating which of Teyseer's 9 verticals the achievement belongs to — enabling users to filter by sector at a glance. This vertical tagging pattern is a direct adaptation of Al Futtaim's category labels on each highlight card.

alfuttaim.com/impact Visit Source
Industry Verticals Section
Al Futtaim — "Our Divisions" typographic accordion list

The Industry Verticals section in v2 uses an expandable accordion layout benchmarked directly from Al Futtaim's "Our Divisions" page. Large bold division names separated by horizontal rules create a typographic list that communicates scale and prestige. Clicking a row expands to reveal company chips, partner brands, and an Explore CTA — Teyseer's adaptation adds richer detail to the Al Futtaim pattern while preserving the bold typographic rhythm.

alfuttaim.com Visit Source
End of Page CTA
Aramco — Full-bleed image CTA with centred headline

The end-of-page CTA in v2 mirrors the Aramco full-bleed CTA benchmark. In v2 the section is split into two columns — a headline + description on the left and two action cards (Partner with Us, Latest News) on the right — adapting Aramco's cinematic full-bleed format into a more structured, scannable layout while preserving the atmospheric, full-width visual weight of the original benchmark.

Teyseer Automotive — Version 1 Open Page

6 section benchmarks across hero, services, business units, and marques.

Our Services Section
Rolls-Royce — "Products & Services" 3-column image card grid

The Our Services section layout in Automotive v1 was benchmarked from Rolls-Royce's Products & Services page. The pattern uses three equal-width, dark-toned image panels each bearing a category icon and label at the bottom-left — letting photography communicate the service category while maintaining a clean, prestige feel. Teyseer adapts this into blue-tinted image cards with service title and description overlaid.

rolls-royce.com Visit Source
Hero Section
Aramco — Full-bleed hero with headline overlay + 3-column editorial text below

The Automotive v1 hero was benchmarked from Aramco's "About Aramco" page. The pattern uses a full-bleed atmospheric photo with a large headline and subtext overlaid in the top-left corner, followed immediately by a 3-column editorial text block below the image — grounding the visual impact with factual copy. Teyseer adapts this into a dark-blue gradient overlay hero with headline, subtext, and a stats strip instead of the 3-column text.

aramco.com Visit Source
Business Units Section
GE Aerospace — "Our portfolio of brands" left text + scrollable tall image cards

The Business Units section in Automotive v1 was first benchmarked from GE Aerospace's brand portfolio layout: a static left column with heading, descriptor, and navigation arrows, paired with a horizontally scrollable set of tall image cards — each bearing a company name, tagline, and arrow CTA at the bottom. Teyseer adapts this format with a vertical card layout per business unit and a fixed 3-column grid rather than a horizontal scroll.

geaerospace.com Visit Source
Business Units Section (Alt)
Al Ghurair — "Innovation Across Sectors" left headline + asymmetric image mosaic

A second benchmark for Business Units, from Al Ghurair's businesses section. The pattern pairs a left-aligned large headline, descriptor, and CTA button with an asymmetric 3-panel image mosaic on the right — each panel overlaid with a category label at the bottom. The "+" explore icon on each image suggests interactivity. This benchmark informed the explore CTA approach and the asymmetric image treatment considered for the Business Units section in v1.

alghurair.com Visit Source
Marques We Represent
Rolls-Royce — "Our Family of Engines" transparent product carousel

The Marques We Represent section carousel in Automotive v1 was benchmarked from Rolls-Royce Civil Aerospace's product carousel. The pattern presents product photography on a white background — no card border or container — with a bold headline link and dot navigation beneath. The clean, product-focused presentation elevates each brand or product. Teyseer adapts this into a horizontally scrollable brand marquee strip for the automotive marques.

rolls-royce.com Visit Source
Marques We Represent (Alt)
Mercedes-Benz — "Our Models" pill tab filter + 3-column product grid

A second benchmark for the Marques section, from Mercedes-Benz MENA's models page. The pattern uses a row of pill-shaped category filter tabs (Sedans, SUVs, Coupés…) above a 3-column product grid — each card showing a product image, model name, price, and a Learn More CTA. This tab-filter + grid approach informed the brand filtering interaction considered for the Marques We Represent section, allowing users to navigate by brand or vehicle category.

Teyseer Automotive — Version 2 Open Page

2 section benchmarks across hero and key projects & accomplishments.

Hero Section
Stripe — Large typographic hero with live stat + brand logo strip

The Automotive v2 hero was benchmarked from Stripe's homepage. The pattern leads with a bold, oversized headline that commands the full width of the viewport, paired with a subtle live-data stat line above it ("Global GDP running on Stripe: X%") and a scrolling strip of trusted partner/client logos below the CTAs. This pattern informed the large-headline hero approach in v2, the stats strip beneath the headline, and the brand marquee running below the hero fold.

stripe.com Visit Source
Key Projects & Accomplishments
Al Futtaim News & Insights — 3-column editorial card grid

The "Key Projects and Accomplishments" section in Automotive v2 was benchmarked from Al Futtaim's News & Insights page. Each card presents a full-bleed image, a category tag (e.g. Financial Services), a date, and a bold multi-line headline — with no body text shown. The editorial card grid pattern communicates credibility and recency. Teyseer adapts this into 4-column cards with a vertical tag, date, headline, source attribution, and a NE-arrow link indicating external news.

alfuttaim.com/news-insights Visit Source

This pattern informed the hover-to-expand behaviour and the image-led visual treatment considered for the accomplishment cards in Teyseer Automotive v2.

Key Accomplishments Section
Siemens — "Global news & press highlights" 3-column image card grid

The Key Accomplishments section in the IT Vertical was benchmarked from Siemens' press page. Each card features a full-bleed image, a bold headline, a location tag, and a short body paragraph — presenting milestones as editorial news items rather than a plain list. Teyseer adapts this into 3-column cards with a vertical category tag, date, headline, one-line description, source attribution, and a NE-arrow link to the original article.

Aramco — "At a glance" minimal large-number stats

The At a Glance stats band in the IT Vertical follows the same Aramco benchmark used across the Industry Verticals pages. Large, light-weight numbers with a short label and a horizontal rule separator communicate scale and credibility with minimal visual decoration. Teyseer's IT Vertical adapts this into a 4-column strip: Years in Qatar, IT Companies, Technology Partners, and Sectors Served.

A second benchmark for the IT Vertical stats strip, from Al Futtaim's Impact page.
